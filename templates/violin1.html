Global Income Inequality

The following section does the exploration of average income per person (measured in terms of GNI per capita income, Atlas method (current US$))of the rich, emerging and poor economies from 1960s until 2015.

Lowest income groups in 1962 and 2015

Which pockets of the world have consistently been on the lower income side ?

Rwanda, Malawi, Nepal, Burundi, Central African Republic, Burkina Faso, Togo, Madagascar have consistently been in low average income group.

In 1962, 6 out 20 countries (India, China, Korean Rep, Nepal, Thailand, Pakistan) in lowest average income group were located in Asia. While in 2015 the only Asian country in lowest average income group happens to be Nepal.In 2015, most of the countries falling in this group are located in African continent and one in middle east.

Did the wealthier nations kept getting rich ?? How about newer candidates now in 2015?

The above graphs and give an interesting information about the rich pockets of the world specially the countries in Western Europe, Scandinavia and North America. These countries had rich economy back in 1960s and are still classified in higher income group in 2015. It shows they had wealth back then and during last 5 decades their wealth kept accumulating as the average income of their citizens kept rising. An important thing is to observe the appearance of newer higher income group countries Qatar, Singapore, Denmark in 2015. Also, it can be duly noted that wealth kept amassing in the developed countries at a higher rate than others.

Rich, emerging and under developed countries from 1962 to 2015

A key points to notice in the above graph is that the growth rate of income in rich countries is much higher than poor countries. The surge in norway's GNI per capita has been very steep after 2002. Also, the growth in poor countries is not steady unlike the richer ones.

Back in 1962, average annual income of Malawi & China were extremely low when compared to United States, Luxembourg, and Norway.The annual average income of a Malawian citizen was only 50 USD which is about 1/6th of a US citizen's income of 3280 USD. It is also worth noticing the value for Malawi in 2015(350) which is still way too less than the average yearly income of a US citizen in 1962. Looks like wealthier nations had a higher leg up than the poor ones... As a matter fact, there is still a considerable difference between the income of several poor countries in 2015 and income of richer countries back in 1962.This leads a conclusion that higher income groups(richer nations) are growing at a faster rate than lower income groups(poorer nations).
